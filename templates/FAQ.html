Frequently Asked Questions about Creating Ideas on IdeaHub



What is stasis theory?

Stasis theory offers a set of questions that creates both an engaging and well designed abstract about your topic. For information about stasis theory, read this article from the Purdue Owl.

Why use an elevator pitch?

An elevator pitch is a short, concise description of a topic that is both engaging to read and clearly explains your idea to your listener. Ensuring that your project pitch is engaging allows for more interest in your project and provides you with more interested applicants.

What is a Conjecture?

Your conjecture should be the background to your summary. In your conjecture you should describe the problem you are addressing and then, briefly, state that your idea as a solution to this problem. Your solution to the problem should be a brief abstract in this section.

What is expected in the quality section of write project

This section should be wear you describe your solution to the problem. It is important that you flesh out your description of the solution and provide specifics to solve your problem. The more specific the better in this section.

How much information should I provide for each section?

The more information the better! The goal of an elevator pitch is to provide as much information as possible while continuing to be engaging. As long as you are not providing useless information (boring specifics or topics that don't apply to your idea) or repeating anything else that you have already said then you are fine.

What kind of images should I provide

Images are important for keeping the pitch engaging, however, they should only be used if they relate to your topics. Quality is much more important than quantity and unecessary pictures will only hurt your project pitch.
